2.2 Layer 2 вҖ” BHA/AHA Active Layer

ComponentConcentrationFunctionpH Target
Salicylic acid (BHA)2.0% w/wOil-soluble keratolytic, COX-2 inhibitor3.5 ± 0.3
Glycolic acid (AHA)5.0% w/wWater-soluble surface exfoliant
Niacinamide (B3)2.0% w/wAnti-inflammatory, barrier repair
Sodium hyaluronate0.5% w/wMoisture retention in channels
Matrix: Hydroxyethyl cellulose gel base (2% HEC in purified water). BHA dissolved in propylene glycol (15% PG co-solvent for oil-soluble SA). pH adjusted with sodium hydroxide 0.1N to 3.5. Sterile-filtered 0.22μm prior to casting onto microneedle base layer.
Critical parameter: pH must remain 3.2-3.8 for BHA/AHA efficacy. Below 3.0 = irritation risk. Above 4.0 = insufficient keratolysis. QC: pH meter every batch + accelerated stability pH drift study.

2.3 Layer 3 вҖ” Hydrocolloid Absorption Matrix

ParameterSpecification

Thickness0.8 mm ± 0.1 mm
Absorption capacity≥15x dry weight in simulated ISF (pH 7.4 PBS)
Absorption rate≥50% capacity within 60 min
Cortisol uptake≥80% of ISF cortisol within 2 hours
Moisture content (as packaged)<5% w/w
Manufacturing: NaCMC/pectin solution cast onto PET release liner, dried at 40°C forced-air oven to target moisture. Cross-linked with 0.5% CaCl2 spray for structural integrity. Die-cut to patch geometry. Laminated onto Layer 2 with pressure-sensitive medical adhesive (acrylate-based, ISO 10993 biocompatible).

7. REGULATORY PATHWAY

Classification: Dual-function product. Skincare component (BHA/AHA/hydrocolloid) = cosmetic. Cortisol diagnostic component = Class II medical device (510(k) pathway, predicate: cortisol diagnostic lateral flow tests). Microneedle component may require separate device review depending on FDA combination product designation.

Recommended path: File as combination product with CDRH (device lead). Request pre-submission meeting to clarify classification. BHA/AHA at cosmetic concentrations (2%/5%) have established safety profiles. Microneedle dissolution safety demonstrated in published literature (Georgia Tech, multiple 2024-2026 studies).

Standards: ISO 10993 (biocompatibility), ISO 13485 (QMS), ISO 11135/11137 (sterilization), IEC 62366 (usability), ISO 14971 (risk management)
